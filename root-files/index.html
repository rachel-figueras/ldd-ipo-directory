Who We Are

Ross Franklin, CEO & COO | Rachel Figueras, CTO & CMO

Both founders are seasoned cannabis entrepreneurs with ten years of cannabis industry experience, from branding & marketing to supply chain management. Prior to founding Little Dragon Dabs™, Ross and Rachel co-invented, patented, and commercially developed a process that turns cannabis oils into a water-soluble powder alongside famed cannabis author and cultivator Ed Rosenthal.

After patenting the original powderization process, they went on to write seven additional USPTO-approved patents related to that technology (one of the first groups of cannabis patents ever issued). From there, they branded the consumer product Stardust by Ed Rosenthal™, commercially produced it in a HACCP-compliant facility, and got it placed in over 100 California dispensaries.

Eventually, Ross and Rachel successfully exited Stardust - the company, trademarks, and patent portfolio were acquired by a publicly-traded Canadian company. In addition to their success in monetizing their intellectual property, both founders are master-level cannabis cultivators, with their original start in cannabis managing and eventually owning large-scale commercial indoor & outdoor grow operations. Post-exit, they successfully navigated the process to obtain a cannabis manufacturer’s license from the city of Costa Mesa and the State of California in 2019.

They each have experience in executive level/upper management careers in the non-cannabis sectors of Architecture & Building Code, Logistics, and International Shipping, and each of them hold two Bachelor’s Degrees in Economics and Public Policy.

Market Opportunity: Dabbers

Dabbers are a young demographic, comprised mainly of millennials and Gen-Zers. These generations place far less value on in-store experiences, opting for convenience and technology instead. As online D2C purchasing behavior continues to steadily grow in the U.S. economy as a whole, no other demographic impacts this rising trend more than young generations.

The dab market is arguably overlooked and underserved by the cannabis industry because most concentrate brands focus on the production of distillates and oils with a heavy reliance on sales of vaping products. Yet remarkably, nationwide data shows dab products (concentrates exclusive of vape products) boast a market size nearly equal to that of edible products. We find this pattern of data revealing – as an opportunity to fill a market void by providing a personalized technology-driven solution, and become the new home for dab culture.

How We Generate Revenue

Pricing | Logistics | Scalability

The company will have significantly lower overhead and operational costs as a delivery-only platform when compared to brick-and-mortar retail dispensaries, and creating contract-manufacturing agreements with top extractors eliminates the need for large capital expenditures when the brand expands into new territory. Logistical advantages come from being nimble and single-category focused, allowing us to provide consumers with the lowest-cost options without sacrificing product quality or healthy profit margins.

The company is built to thrive in challenging economic environments due to its low overhead and low price point. While circumventing the in-store experience with 100% D2C delivery -exclusively available via mobile- the app will continuously analyze customer purchasing patterns alongside product data, enabling the most informed product selections & creating predictable experiences better than a human budtender.

Who can invest in a Regulation D offering?

Anyone, accredited and non-accredited investors alike, are eligible to purchase securities in a Regulation D offering.

What is the difference between an accredited and a non-accredited investor?

An accredited investor is an individual who makes over $200,000 per year ($300,000 if combined with a spouse) or who has a net worth of $1 million or more, excluding their primary residence. A non-accredited investor is anyone who does not meet the net worth requirements for an accredited investor.

How much money can I invest in a Regulation D offering?

Non-accredited investors can invest up to 10% of the greater of their annual income or net worth. There are no restrictions for accredited investors.
